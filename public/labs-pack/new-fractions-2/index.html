Math Lab: Fractions

What are a “whole” and a “fraction”

The square on the board is one whole (number 1). When we split the whole into equal parts, each part is called a fraction. Examples: into 2 parts — each is 1/2; into 4 — 1/4; into 8 — 1/8; into 16 — 1/16, and so on.

Terms

  • whole — the thing we split (here it is the square);
  • part — one of the equal shares of the whole;
  • fraction 1/n — one of n equal parts of the whole (the larger n, the smaller the part);
  • equal parts — same shape and area.

How the “hammer” works

  • Each hit doubles the number of parts, and the hit applies only to the selected part.
  • The direction is set by the “Direction” button. Modifiers: Shift — vertical, Ctrl — horizontal, right‑click — opposite to the current one. No auto switching.

Examples

  • Need 4 equal parts — make 2 hits (2, then 4 parts).
  • Need the fraction 1/8 — split one part 3 times to get a 1/8 piece.
  • Need the fraction 1/64 — split one part 6 times to get a 1/64 piece.

Tips

  • If you have fewer parts than required — keep hitting the hammer.
  • If you got too many parts — press “Clear board” and start again.
  • Ready? Press “Submit for check” — the teacher will respond and give a new task.
