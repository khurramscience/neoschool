Understanding Multiplication by 10

🎯 Key Concept

When we multiply a number by 10, we add one zero to the right of that number.

Examples:

5 × 10 = 50 Add one zero: 5 → 50
23 × 10 = 230 Add one zero: 23 → 230
156 × 10 = 1,560 Add one zero: 156 → 1,560

🚀 Multiple Multiplications by 10

When we multiply by 10 multiple times, we add the same number of zeros:

Multiplying by 10 twice:

7 × 10 × 10 = 7 × 100 = 700 Add two zeros: 7 → 700

Multiplying by 10 three times:

3 × 10 × 10 × 10 = 3 × 1,000 = 3,000 Add three zeros: 3 → 3,000
