Meet the founder

After considering traditional and alternative Kindergarten options for my son, I realized there was an opportunity to create a microschool where kids learn core academics at their own level and pace, and life skills that build real-world confidence and independence.

So I invited experienced educators (including Esther Wojcicki, educator and author of How to Raise Successful People), a community of families, and seasoned edtech entrepreneurs — the content providers behind AI tutors used by millions of students — to co-build the school we've all been dreaming of.

The first one opens in Missoula, where my kids are growing up as second generation Montanans. We'd love for your family to be part of it.

— Jennie Yang

Frequently asked questions

Good questions, honest answers

Click any question. If yours isn't here, email Jennie.

Who is neoschool for?
neoschool Missoula is built for curious Kindergarten and 1st grade students whose families want personalized academics, hands-on life skills, and meaningful outdoor time — without the constraints of traditional school. We welcome a mix of children new to school and those transitioning from public, private, homeschool, or preschool settings.
How is neoschool different from traditional school?
Three key differences: (1) Two-hour personalized academic block instead of all-day desk learning, where AI-adaptive tools meet each child at their level. (2) Daily life-skills workshops — cooking, financial literacy, conscious communication, gardening — that traditional schools rarely teach. (3) Real outdoor play and forest days, because attention and learning improve when kids move and get fresh air.
What does a typical day look like?
30 minutes of morning play → 2 hours of personalized academics (9:00–11:00am) → 1.5 hours outdoor play and lunch (11:00am–12:30pm) → 3 hours of real projects and life skills (12:30–3:30pm) → 1 hour outdoor play (3:30–4:30pm). Brain breaks every 15–20 minutes during academic blocks.
Is this a microschool? Are you accredited?
Yes — neoschool Missoula operates as a private microschool. Montana's regulatory framework supports private schools and microschools, and we comply with state requirements. Accreditation is a multi-year process we will pursue as the school matures. Our educational outcomes are tracked rigorously through our content providers (used by 1000+ schools) and presented to families in clear progress reports.
What's the student-to-teacher ratio?
10–12 students per class with mixed-age learning (Kindergarten + 1st grade together). At least one lead educator and one assistant per classroom, ensuring meaningful 1:1 attention. Small enough to know every child deeply.
What curriculum do you use?
A blend of best-in-class providers: AI-adaptive math (Zearn, Synthesis Tutor), reading (Amira Learning, CommonLit), hands-on science (PhET, Mystery Science), coding (Scratch, Code.org), and original neoschool project-based learning units. Everything aligns with Common Core and the Montana Content Standards, but we go far beyond them.
Do you offer financial aid or sibling discounts?
As a founding cohort program, we're committed to keeping neoschool accessible. We offer limited need-based financial aid and sibling discounts. Please mention your situation in the application and Jennie will discuss options personally.
Is lunch provided? What about snacks?
Families pack lunches and morning snacks. We're working on a healthy lunch program for the 2027–2028 school year. Garden produce from our on-campus garden (which kids help plant and tend) supplements snack time.
What if my child has learning differences?
neoschool's adaptive academic model is naturally suited to children with diverse learning profiles. Our 1:1 AI tutoring meets kids exactly where they are, and our 10–12 student classroom allows for individualized support. We're not a special-education program — but for many neurodivergent kids, our approach works far better than traditional school. Let's discuss your child during the admissions meeting.
Can homeschool families drop in part-time?
Yes — we offer drop-in half days for homeschooling families. This is a great way to access community, life-skills workshops, and outdoor play without a full enrollment commitment. Contact us for half-day pricing.
When and where will the campus open?
Opening September 2026 in Missoula, Montana. Final location is being secured now and will be communicated to applying families. We are exploring options in central Missoula with outdoor space and easy access to natural areas.
What if I miss the application deadline?
We accept applications on a rolling basis until our founding cohort is full. Apply as soon as you can — early applicants have the best chance at securing a spot for September 2026.
