02 · The philosophy

Built for how kids actually learn.

Not how schools have always been organized — how learning actually happens. Two focused hours of academics. Three hours of real projects. Daily outdoor play. AI tutors that adapt. Educators that connect.

Personalized academics

Two focused hours

AI-adaptive learning blended with proven classroom practices: peer instruction, small-group activities, real-time educator feedback. Brain breaks between subjects, never longer than 18 minutes at a stretch.

Real-world projects

Make, build, present

Hands-on projects that connect what they're learning to the world outside the classroom. Cooking introduces fractions. Gardens introduce biology. Entrepreneurship introduces math.

Life skills workshops

What school usually skips

Cooking, gardening, survival skills, financial literacy, public speaking, entrepreneurship, emotion regulation. Local Missoula experts teach what matters in the real world.

Outdoor play + sports

Unstructured time outside

Daily forest days. Movement-based learning. The research is unambiguous: 3+ hours of outdoor play measurably improves attention, attendance, and school readiness.

03 · A typical day

From 8:30am to 4:30pm.

Long enough that working families don't need separate childcare. Structured enough that kids feel rhythm and progress. Loose enough that play, curiosity, and life happen.

8:30 — 9:00am

Morning play

Arrival window. Free play, conversation, breakfast. Settling into the day before academics begin.

9:00 — 11:00am

Personalized academics

Two hours of rotation: small-group instruction, AI-adaptive practice, hands-on activities. Reading, writing, math, science. Brain breaks every 15–20 minutes.

11:00 — 12:30pm

Outdoor play + lunch

Outside first, lunch second. Movement before food. Built around what we know about attention and learning.

12:30 — 3:30pm

Real projects + life skills

Three hours of project-based learning and weekly workshops. This is what kids will remember decades later.

3:30 — 4:30pm

Outdoor play

End the day moving. Sports, free play, forest exploration. Working parents pick up at 4:30 or by arrangement.

The neoschool model

Is the neoschool model proven?
Yes. The 2-hour academics model is used by microschools nationally with strong outcomes on third-party assessments. The AI tutoring component is backed by a 2025 Harvard RCT (Kestin et al., Scientific Reports) showing AI tutoring produces 2× learning gains vs. best-practice classroom instruction.
Will kids be sitting on screens for 2 hours straight?
Not at all. The 2-hour academic block is short, mastery-based cycles — typically 10–15 minutes per subject, with brain breaks and movement in between. Screens are one tool among many.
How long is the neoschool day?
8:30am–4:30pm, fully included in tuition. Working-family friendly.

Academics

Do students still learn reading, math, and science?
Absolutely. Reading, writing, math, and science are the foundation of the 2-hour daily academic block. What's different is how: AI tutors adapt to each child's exact level and pace.
Do students really learn more in only 2 hours a day?
The research says yes. A 2025 Harvard RCT found AI tutoring produces 2× learning gains compared to best-practice classroom instruction.

AI & technology

Is the AI tutor replacing the teacher?
No. For K and 1st grade, neoschool is instructor-led first. Educators are in the room every day, leading small-group instruction. The AI tutor supplements — freeing educators to spend more time mentoring and connecting.
How does neoschool approach AI safety for young children?
Thoughtfully. We use age-appropriate, education-specific AI tools — not general-purpose chatbots. Screen time is intentional and time-limited. We follow COPPA guidelines.

Tuition & enrollment

How much does it cost to apply?
Nothing. Applying is completely free.
What does tuition cover?
The complete school day (8:30am–4:30pm), all academic instruction, AI tutor access, life skills workshops, healthy snacks, sports, forest days, field trips, materials, and the neoschool digital platform. No hidden fees.
Do you offer part-time for homeschooling families?
Yes — half-day drop-in enrollment includes life skills workshops, outdoor play, and the neoschool community. Reach out for details.
