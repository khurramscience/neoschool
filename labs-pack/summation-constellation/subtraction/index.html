📚 Subtraction Theory

What is subtraction?

Subtraction is a mathematical operation that shows how much one number is less than another or how much remains after removing a part from a whole.

Example: 15 - 7 = 8

This means that if we have 15 objects and we remove 7, then 8 objects remain.

Column subtraction algorithm

  1. Write the numbers one under the other, aligned by digits
  2. Start subtraction from right to left (from ones)
  3. If the digit of the minuend is less than the digit of the subtrahend, borrow from the higher digit
  4. Continue to the leftmost digit
1 2 5
- 8 7
0 3 8

Special cases

Subtraction with borrowing

When the digit in the minuend is less than the digit in the subtrahend, you need to borrow 1 from the higher digit.

52 - 7 = 45 (borrow 1 from tens)

Subtracting zero

When subtracting zero, the number does not change.

25 - 0 = 25

Subtracting from zero

From zero, you can only subtract zero, the result will be zero.

0 - 0 = 0
